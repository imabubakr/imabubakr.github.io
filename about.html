About Me

My journey in GIS and Remote Sensing started during my undergraduate studies in Geology, where I discovered the potential of spatial data to address real-world challenges. During my graduate studies, I focused on building advanced skills in GIS and Remote Sensing by taking courses in image analysis, geospatial statistics, and spatial databases. I also worked on various research projects using Earth Observation data to investigate the impact of climate variability on our planet.

I have been fortunate to work on a wide range of exciting projects that have challenged me to push my skills to new limits. My experience includes conducting GIS and Remote Sensing analysis, managing and organizing spatial data in Geodatabases, designing and developing geospatial models, creating custom maps, and more. I have worked with clients from various industries, including urban planning, natural resource management, and academia, among others. These projects have given me the opportunity to apply my skills in innovative ways and to develop custom solutions to complex spatial problems.

In addition to my work in traditional GIS and Remote Sensing, I am also passionate about exploring the potential of machine learning and deep learning in spatial science. In fact, during my free time, I enjoy working on hobby projects that involve building machine learning models for spatial data analysis. Moreover, I have a keen interest in data visualization, open data, and emerging technologies in the field. I am constantly exploring new tools and techniques to improve my work and stay at the forefront of the field.
