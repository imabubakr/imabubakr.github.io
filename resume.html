Academics

  • Glacier Mass Balance

    During my geological project, I employed a range of datasets such as old historical imageries, Landsat 5, 7 and 8, Sentinel 2, and DEMs to examine the mass balance of Mýrdalsjökull and its sublet glaciers over the past four decades. At first, band ratios were utilized for snow index calculations, but the outcomes proved to be insufficient. To address this, the main glacier and its sublet glaciers were manually digitized for different years and their respective areas and volumes were calculated. Through our analysis, we observed a twofold increase in the melting rate after the year 2000, indicating a considerable impact of climate change on the glacier. This project provided me with a more comprehensive understanding of the significance of geospatial data analysis in monitoring and responding to the effects of climate change.

  • Snow Cover and Precipitation Quantification

    As part of my semester research work, I utilized MODIS and TRMM datasets to quantify snow cover and average precipitation between the years 2000 and 2015. The normalized snow index derived from MODIS data was used to estimate the snow cover, while the average rainfall was determined by converting the monthly product of TRMM from mm/h to mm/month. Although no significant change was observed in the snow cover initially, a gradual decrease was noted in the last 3-4 years, possibly due to changes in snow accumulation periods. Moreover, the rainfall data showed variations, with lower precipitation levels in the early years and the highest levels recorded in 2010. This project not only allowed me to enhance my skills in GIS data analysis but also highlighted the importance of monitoring climate-related phenomena to address the challenges posed by climate change.

  • Hydrological Modeling

    I utilized Landsat 7 data spanning six years to calculate the snow index for every three-month period and track changes in snow cover area over time. This project helped me develop my skills in remote sensing and provided me with valuable insights into the importance of snowpack monitoring in water resource management.

  • Mapping Volcanic Terrain

    In my mapping volcanic terrain project, I utilized Landsat 8 and ASTER DEM datasets to analyze the volcanic landcover, erosion, symmetry, morphometry, and growth rate of Mauna Kea. The Landsat 8 dataset was used to create a landcover map of the volcano, while the ASTER DEM dataset was used to analyze the terrain morphology and assess the growth rate of the volcano. Through this project, I was able to gain expertise in remote sensing and GIS analysis techniques, and developed a deeper understanding of the geological processes that shape volcanic landscapes.
