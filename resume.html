Class Projects

  • Glacier Mass Balance

    During my geological project, I employed a range of datasets such as old historical imageries, Landsat 5, 7 and 8, Sentinel 2, and DEMs to examine the mass balance of Mýrdalsjökull and its sublet glaciers over the past four decades. At first, band ratios were utilized for snow index calculations, but the outcomes proved to be insufficient. To address this, the main glacier and its sublet glaciers were manually digitized for different years and their respective areas and volumes were calculated. Through our analysis, we observed a twofold increase in the melting rate after the year 2000, indicating a considerable impact of climate change on the glacier.

  • Snow Cover and Precipitation Quantification

    As part of my semester research work, I utilized MODIS and TRMM datasets to quantify snow cover and average precipitation between the years 2000 and 2015. The normalized snow index derived from MODIS data was used to estimate the snow cover, while the average rainfall was determined by converting the monthly product of TRMM from mm/h to mm/month. Although no significant change was observed in the snow cover initially, a gradual decrease was noted in the last 3-4 years, possibly due to changes in snow accumulation periods. Moreover, the rainfall data showed variations, with lower precipitation levels in the early years and the highest levels recorded in 2010.

  • Hydrological Modeling

    I employed Landsat 7 data to calculate the snow index for every four-month period. This analysis allowed me to track changes in snow cover area over time, providing valuable insights into the dynamics of snow cover in the Kabul Basin region.

  • Mapping Volcanic Terrain

    In my mapping volcanic terrain project, I utilized Landsat 8 and ASTER DEM datasets to analyze the volcanic landcover, erosion, symmetry, morphometry, and growth rate of Mauna Kea. The Landsat 8 dataset was used to create a landcover map of the volcano, while the ASTER DEM dataset was used to analyze the terrain morphology and assess the growth rate of the volcano.
